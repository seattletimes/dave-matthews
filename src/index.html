Explore Dave Matthews Band’s studio albums

Dave Matthews Band’s second year of Rock & Roll Hall of Fame eligibility comes 25 years after the release of its first major-label album, “Under the Table and Dreaming.” The trade of bootleg concert recordings among fans had elevated Dave Matthews Band to idol status in the ‘90s, but it’s the band’s nine studio albums that likely caught the Rock Hall’s eye. The band’s range can be seen in its albums, from the folk-rock spirit of “Crash”, to the elaborate orchestral arrangements on “Big Whiskey & the GrooGrux King.” DMB’s seven most recent studio albums debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard charts, making Dave Matthews Band the group with the most No. 1 consecutive debuts, topped only by solo acts Eminem and Kanye West.
